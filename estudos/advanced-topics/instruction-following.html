Instruction Following

Seguimento de Instruções em IA

Instruction following (seguimento de instruções) is a core evaluation criterion (critério de avaliação) in artificial intelligence systems, particularly in language models. It measures whether the model correctly interprets a prompt (instrução) and produces outputs (saídas) that follow the required format, constraints, and context.

In practice, human evaluators assess whether outputs comply with established guidelines (diretrizes) and meet defined evaluation standards. This includes verifying factual accuracy, completeness, relevance, and adherence to instructions. Even minor deviations, such as ignoring constraints or partially answering the prompt, are considered failures.

Models may generate outputs that appear plausible but do not fully follow the instruction. These cases often involve incomplete reasoning, incorrect assumptions, or missing constraints. Such issues reduce reliability and negatively impact downstream tasks such as annotation, ranking, and validation.

Inconsistent instruction following leads to variability across outputs, making it harder to maintain quality and standardisation. Over time, these inconsistencies can affect model performance and reduce user trust.

For this reason, combining clear guidelines (diretrizes), well-defined evaluation criteria, and continuous human review is essential. This process improves output consistency, reduces errors, and ensures better alignment between model behaviour and user intent.
