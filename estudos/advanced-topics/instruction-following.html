Instruction Following

Seguimento de Instruções em IA

Instruction following is a core evaluation criterion (avaliação) in artificial intelligence systems, particularly in language models. It measures whether a model correctly understands and responds to a prompt (instrução), while respecting format, constraints, and context.

In practice, human evaluators assess whether responses follow established guidelines (diretrizes) and meet defined evaluation standards. This includes checking instruction adherence, factual accuracy, completeness, and relevance to the task objective.

Common failures occur when models ignore constraints, misinterpret instructions, or produce partially relevant responses. These issues directly impact the quality of outputs (saídas) and reduce reliability in annotation (anotação) and evaluation workflows.

Inconsistent instruction following leads to misaligned results, reduced user trust, and lower performance in real-world applications. Even when responses appear plausible, lack of alignment with the prompt can compromise usability and decision-making.

To mitigate these risks, clear guidelines (diretrizes), well-defined evaluation criteria, and continuous human review are essential. This combination improves output consistency, reduces errors, and ensures alignment between model responses and user intent.

Source: AWS Responsible AI Practices (2026); instruction-following evaluation research (2025).
